Predicting automobile CO² emissions

This project involves building a predictive model to estimate the amount of CO2 emitted by automobiles. The model is based on a large database from a Canadian dealership that describes various characteristics of hundreds of cars. The goal is to create an accurate and reliable model that can be used to predict CO2 emissions for different types of automobiles. The model was constructed using a combination of statistical techniques, including multiple regression analysis and data transformation methods.

Estimating retail sales through a time series

The intention here was to create an accurate model using a time series from IBGE that can be used to predict the volume of future retail sales in the state of Minas Gerais. The time series in question was analyzed and modeled using statistical techniques, including ARIMA and ETS modeling, in order to capture different aspects of it. In the end, it was possible to validate the quality of the forecasts through performance metrics such as Mean Absolute Error (MAE).

Ranking RMs through Factor Analysis

With the rapid growth of large Brazilian cities, the phenomenon of conurbation has led to the formation of several metropolitan regions (RMs) throughout the country. The Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics (IBGE) provides an extensive database with information on the level of development of the main metropolitan regions in Brazil. In order to simplify this information and gain a broader perspective, we will perform a Principal Component Factor Analysis. This analysis will allow us to reduce the complexity of the database into meaningful factors that will be used to classify the metropolitan regions according to their level of development.

Correspondence analysis for cardiac patients

Correspondence analysis is a technique that allows the assessment of the association between qualitative variables and their categories. It is also capable of condensing the information from these variables into numerical values that can be plotted in various types of graphs or even used in other analytical models. In this project, we will analyze a database containing cardiovascular information from hundreds of medical patients to understand which characteristics may be associated with heart diseases.
